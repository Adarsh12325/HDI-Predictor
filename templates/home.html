Human Development Index

Empowering socioeconomic progress analysis using Machine Learning

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a statistic composite index of life expectancy, education, and per capita income indicators, which are used to rank countries into four development tiers: Very High, High, Medium, and Low. A country scores a higher HDI when the lifespan is higher, the education level is higher, and the gross national income GNI (PPP) per capita is higher. The HDI was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, not economic growth alone.

This web application implements a Linear Regression machine learning model to predict a country's HDI score based on key national socioeconomic indicators. Enter specific dimension measurements to find the predicted score, determine its development tier, and discover the weakest dimension constraint.
